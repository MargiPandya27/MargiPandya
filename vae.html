Variational Autoencoder (VAE): Likelihood Estimation

Overview

A Variational Autoencoder (VAE) is a generative model that learns a probabilistic mapping between observed data and a latent space. VAEs are widely used for generating complex data such as images, text, and audio.

Motivation

Given a dataset (e.g., faces), a VAE learns to encode each data point into a latent representation. By sampling from this latent space and decoding, the model can generate new, realistic samples.

For data \( X \), the goal is to maximize the marginal likelihood:
\[ p(x) = \int p(x, z) \, dz \] where \( z \) is the latent variable.

This integral is generally intractable, so we approximate it using sampling techniques.

Sampling Techniques

1. Monte Carlo Sampling

Approximate the integral by averaging over samples from the latent space:

\[ p(x) \approx \frac{1}{k} \sum_{i=1}^{k} p(x, z^{(i)}), \quad z^{(i)} \sim \text{Uniform} \]

2. Importance Sampling

Sample \( z \) from a proposal distribution \( q(z) \) that is closer to the true posterior:

\[ p(x) = \int \frac{p(x, z)}{q(z)} q(z) \, dz = \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q(z)}\left[\frac{p(x, z)}{q(z)}\right] \] \[ \approx \frac{1}{k} \sum_{i=1}^{k} \frac{p(x, z^{(i)})}{q(z^{(i)})}, \quad z^{(i)} \sim q(z) \]
Note: In VAEs, the proposal distribution \( q(z) \) is replaced by the encoder \( q_\phi(z|x) \).

Likelihood Estimator and ELBO Derivation

For a single data point \( x \) and parameters \( \theta \), the marginal likelihood is:

\[ p(x; \theta) = \int p(x, z; \theta) \, dz \]

For a dataset \( X = \{x^{(1)}, \dots, x^{(m)}\} \), the total likelihood is:

\[ p(X; \theta) = \prod_{i=1}^{m} p(x^{(i)}; \theta) \quad \Rightarrow \quad \log p(X; \theta) = \sum_{i=1}^{m} \log p(x^{(i)}; \theta) \]

Step-by-Step Derivation:

  1. Rewrite using importance sampling:
    \[ \log p(x; \theta) = \log \left( \int q_\phi(z|x) \cdot \frac{p(x, z)}{q_\phi(z|x)} \, dz \right) = \log \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)} \left[ \frac{p(x, z)}{q_\phi(z|x)} \right] \]
  2. Apply Jensen’s inequality (log is concave):
    \[ \log \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)} \left[ \frac{p(x, z)}{q_\phi(z|x)} \right] \geq \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)} \left[ \log \left( \frac{p(x, z)}{q_\phi(z|x)} \right) \right] \]
  3. Define the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO):
    \[ \mathcal{L}(x; \theta, \phi) = \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)} \left[ \log p_\theta(x|z) \right] - D_{KL}(q_\phi(z|x) \| p(z)) \]
  4. Condition for equality: The inequality becomes equality when \( q_\phi(z|x) = p(z|x) \), the true posterior.

VAE Architecture

  • Encoder: Learns \( q_\phi(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\phi(x), \sigma_\phi^2(x)I) \) via a neural network.
  • Decoder: Models \( p_\theta(x|z) \), typically as a Gaussian or Bernoulli distribution.
  • Prior: Usually \( p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, I) \).

For images: Encoders use convolutional layers; decoders use transposed convolutions.

KL Divergence (Gaussian Case)

For \( q_\phi(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\phi(x), \sigma_\phi^2(x)I) \) and prior \( p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, I) \), the KL divergence has a closed form:

\[ D_{\text{KL}}(q_\phi(z|x) \| p(z)) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{j=1}^d \left( -\log \sigma_{\phi,j}^2(x) + \mu_{\phi,j}^2(x) + \sigma_{\phi,j}^2(x) - 1 \right) \]

Reparameterization Trick

To enable backpropagation through stochastic sampling, we use:

\[ z = \mu_\phi(x) + \sigma_\phi(x) \cdot \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I) \]

Reconstruction Loss Gradient Derivation

\[ \nabla_\phi \mathbb{E}_{q_\phi(z|x)}[\log p_\theta(x|z)] = \nabla_\phi \int q_\phi(z|x) \log p_\theta(x|z) dz \] Assuming interchange of gradient and integral: \[ = \int \log p_\theta(x|z) \nabla_\phi q_\phi(z|x) dz \] Using the identity: \[ \nabla_\phi q_\phi(z|x) = q_\phi(z|x) \nabla_\phi \log q_\phi(z|x) \] So, \[ = \int \log p_\theta(x|z) q_\phi(z|x) \nabla_\phi \log q_\phi(z|x) dz = \mathbb{E}_{q_\phi(z|x)}[\log p_\theta(x|z) \nabla_\phi \log q_\phi(z|x)] \]

Training & Generation

  1. Encode \( x \) to get \( \mu_\phi(x), \sigma_\phi(x) \).
  2. Sample \( z \) via the reparameterization trick.
  3. Decode \( z \) to reconstruct \( x \).
  4. Optimize ELBO: maximize reconstruction, minimize KL divergence.
Generation: Sample \( z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I) \), decode to get a new sample \( x_{\text{new}} \).

Summary

  • VAEs maximize a lower bound (ELBO) on data likelihood.
  • They use neural networks to approximate the intractable posterior.
  • The reparameterization trick enables efficient training.
  • KL divergence regularizes the latent space.
