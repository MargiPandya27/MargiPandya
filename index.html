    Margi Pandya

    MS Student @ UC San Diego | Multimodal Learning · Vision & Language · Generative AI · Semantic Segmentation

    I’m a graduate student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UC San Diego, focused on building intelligent AI systems at the intersection of computer vision, multimodal learning, and generative models. My research and project work span across amodal segmentation, diffusion-based image completion, and retrieval-augmented generation.

    I’ve conducted research at UCSD's Statistical Visual Computing Lab on occlusion reasoning via natural language prompts, where I evaluated multiple vision-language models and designed multimodal pipelines for image completion. At IIT Kanpur, I developed ContraFusionNet—a CNN-Transformer hybrid segmentation network with knowledge distillation and contrastive loss, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in low-data regimes.

    Previously at NIT Trichy, I worked on multi-object co-segmentation, engineered a custom Cityscapes-based dataset, and automated training pipelines to improve efficiency by over 30%. I also led the ML team at DataByte, built AI-based health and safety systems, and organized educational workshops for over 100+ students.

    I’ve built AI applications ranging from LLM-driven campaign creators and PDF summarization using RAG to edge detection via fuzzy logic and low-cost pest detection systems on Raspberry Pi. My tech stack includes PyTorch, TensorFlow, OpenCV, HuggingFace, LangChain, and cloud tools like AWS and GitHub Actions.

    I'm currently seeking full-time or internship opportunities in machine learning research, AI engineering, or multimodal systems where I can apply my skills in generative modeling, vision-language learning, and scalable AI deployment.
