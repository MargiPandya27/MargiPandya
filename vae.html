Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)

Goal

We want to model the data likelihood \( p(x) \) using latent variables \( z \) by introducing a joint distribution \( p(x, z) \) such that:

\[ p(x) = \int p(x|z)p(z)\,dz \]

This integral is usually intractable, so we approximate it using techniques like Monte Carlo sampling or importance sampling.

Monte Carlo Estimation

If we could sample from the prior \( z \sim p(z) \), we could estimate \( p(x) \) using:

\[ p(x) \approx \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N p(x|z^{(i)}), \quad \text{where } z^{(i)} \sim p(z) \]

However, this typically gives poor estimates because most samples will be in low-density regions of \( p(x|z) \). So we use importance sampling with a more tractable distribution \( q(z|x) \).

Importance Sampling and ELBO

We reweight the integrand using a variational distribution \( q(z|x) \):

\[ p(x) = \int \frac{p(x, z)}{q(z|x)} q(z|x) \, dz = \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q(z|x)} \left[ \frac{p(x, z)}{q(z|x)} \right] \]

Applying Jensen’s inequality:

\[ \log p(x) \geq \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q(z|x)} \left[ \log \frac{p(x, z)}{q(z|x)} \right] \]

This is the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO), which we maximize during training:

\[ \text{ELBO}(x) = \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q(z|x)}[\log p(x|z)] - D_{\text{KL}}(q(z|x) \| p(z)) \]

Loss Function in VAEs

Our objective is to maximize the ELBO, or equivalently, minimize the negative ELBO:

\[ \mathcal{L} = -\mathbb{E}_{z \sim q(z|x)}[\log p(x|z)] + D_{\text{KL}}(q(z|x) \| p(z)) \]

KL Divergence Between Gaussians

Assume:

  • Prior: \( p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, I) \)
  • Encoder output: \( q(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu(x), \text{diag}(\sigma^2(x))) \)

The KL divergence between two Gaussians has a closed form:

\[ D_{\text{KL}}(\mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2 I) \| \mathcal{N}(0, I)) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{j=1}^{d} \left( \mu_j^2 + \sigma_j^2 - \log \sigma_j^2 - 1 \right) \]

Reparameterization Trick

To backpropagate through \( z \sim q(z|x) \), we reparameterize the sampling process:

\[ z = \mu(x) + \sigma(x) \cdot \epsilon, \quad \text{where } \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I) \]

This makes the sampling differentiable with respect to the encoder parameters.

Summary

  • VAEs model \( p(x) \) using a latent variable \( z \).
  • We use ELBO to make learning tractable.
  • The encoder outputs \( \mu(x), \sigma(x) \) to define a Gaussian \( q(z|x) \).
  • The decoder reconstructs \( x \) from \( z \), and loss combines reconstruction and KL terms.
