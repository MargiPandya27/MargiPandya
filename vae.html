Variational Autoencoder (VAE)

Instead of maximizing the probability of a point x directly, we maximize its log-likelihood:

\[ \log p(x) = \log \int p(x|z) p(z) \; dz \]

In VAEs, we introduce an approximate posterior \( q(z|x) \) to turn this into a lower bound (ELBO):

\[ \log p(x) \geq \mathbb{E}_{q(z|x)} [\log p(x|z)] - \mathrm{KL}(q(z|x) || p(z)) \]

Sampling from a Multivariate Gaussian

To sample \( z \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma) \), we use:

\[ z = \mu + A \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I) \]

Here, \( A \) is a matrix such that \( AA^T = \Sigma \), typically found via Cholesky decomposition.

ELBO Derivation

We start from:

\[ \log p(x) = \log \int p(x, z) \; dz = \log \int q(z|x) \frac{p(x,z)}{q(z|x)} \; dz \]

Then use Jensen’s Inequality:

\[ \log p(x) \geq \mathbb{E}_{q(z|x)} \left[ \log \frac{p(x,z)}{q(z|x)} \right] = \text{ELBO} \]

Reparameterization Trick

We reparameterize \( z \sim q(z|x) \) as:

\[ z = \mu(x) + \sigma(x) \cdot \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I) \]

This allows gradient flow through \( \mu \) and \( \sigma \) during backpropagation.

KL Divergence Between Two Gaussians

Given \( q(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2) \) and \( p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0,1) \), the KL divergence is:

\[ \mathrm{KL}(q || p) = -\frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{d} \left(1 + \log \sigma_i^2 - \mu_i^2 - \sigma_i^2\right) \]
