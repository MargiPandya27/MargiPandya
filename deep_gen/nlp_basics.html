    NLP Preprocessing Concepts

    1. Tokenization

    A token is the building unit of a text or sentence. Multiple tokens combine to form a sentence, and multiple sentences form a paragraph.

    Different types of tokenization:

    • Character Tokenization:
      “We love NLP!” ⇒ ‘W’, ‘e’, ‘l’, ‘o’, ‘v’, ‘e’, ‘N’, ’L’, ’P’, ’!’
    • Word Tokenization:
      “We love NLP!” ⇒ ‘We’, ‘love’, ‘NLP’, ‘!’
    • Sentence Tokenization:
      “We love NLP! I should work hard.” ⇒ ‘We love NLP!’, ‘I should work hard.’

    Common libraries: re, nltk, scapy

    2. Stemming

    Stemming extracts the root form (stem) of words. For example:

    • goes, go, going ⇒ go
    • history, historical ⇒ histori

    3. Lemmatization

    Lemmatization also reduces words to their root form but retains the actual dictionary form (lemma), preserving the meaning.

    • history, historical ⇒ history

    4. Stopwords

    Stopwords are common words that do not carry significant meaning and are removed to reduce noise.

    Examples: “is”, “a”, “the”, etc.

    5. Case Sensitivity

    Machines treat ‘He’ and ‘he’ as different tokens. Normalize case (e.g., to lowercase) unless the case is important (e.g., for proper nouns).

    6. Bag-of-Words (BoW)

    BoW represents text as the frequency of words after preprocessing (tokenization, stopword removal, etc.).

    Example Sentences:

    • Sentence 1: He is a good boy
    • Sentence 2: She is a good girl
    • Sentence 3: Boy and girl are good.

    Binary BoW:

    good girl boy
    Sentence 1 1 0 1
    Sentence 2 1 1 0
    Sentence 3 1 1 1

    Numerical BoW:

    • Sentence 1: He is a good boy
    • Sentence 2: She is a good girl
    • Sentence 3: Girls are in general good than boys. Boy and girl are good.
    good girl boy
    Sentence 1 1 0 1
    Sentence 2 1 1 0
    Sentence 3 2 2 2

    Note: BoW does not capture semantic information; it just counts the frequency of words.

    7. Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF)

    Term Frequency (TF):

    good girl boy
    Sentence 1 1/2 0 1/2
    Sentence 2 1/2 1/2 0
    Sentence 3 1/3 1/3 1/3

    Inverse Document Frequency (IDF):

    Term IDF
    good log(3/3)
    girl log(3/2)
    boy log(3/2)

    TF-IDF Scores:

    good girl boy
    Sentence 1 0 0 1/2 * log(3/2)
    Sentence 2 0 1/2 * log(3/2) 0
    Sentence 3 0 1/3 * log(3/2) 1/3 * log(3/2)

    TF-IDF helps in identifying the most relevant words in a document. It doesn’t capture semantic relationships but emphasizes word importance across documents.
