    Margi Pandya

    MS Student @ UC San Diego | Generative AI · Multimodal Learning · Vision & Language

    Hi! I’m a Master’s student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UC San Diego, specializing in machine learning for computer vision, language, and multimodal systems. I am deeply interested in building intelligent, scalable AI models that perceive and reason like humans.

    I briefly worked at UCSD’s Statistical Visual Computing Lab, where I focused on amodal segmentation and image completion. My research aimed to reconstruct occluded or missing visual content using diffusion models and vision-language grounding. As a result, my current research interests span:

    1. Generative AI approaches, including diffusion models and large language models for image generation and understanding.
    2. Development of vision-language models (VLMs) for robust multimodal learning, especially under limited-data regimes.
    3. Weakly supervised and low-data learning techniques for scene understanding through semantic segmentation and object detection.

    My undergraduate thesis focused on multi-foreground co-segmentation of objects in a given set of images at NIT Trichy, under the guidance of Dr. Avik Hati. Previously, at the Perception and Intelligence Lab, I developed ContraFusionNet at IIT Kanpur—a semantic segmentation model that combines CNNs and Transformers in a contrastive distillation framework, achieving high accuracy with limited data.

    I'm currently seeking co-op/internship opportunities in machine learning and AI engineering, where I can apply my skills in generative modeling, vision-language learning, and scalable AI deployment.
