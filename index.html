    Research Projects

    1. Multiple Foreground Co-segmentation
      Advisor: Dr. Avik Hati
      This project focuses on multiple foreground common object co-segmentation, a computer vision task with applications in image understanding and object recognition. The goal is to segment common objects shared across a group of images. While most prior work has focused on single foreground co-segmentation, we explore techniques to identify and segment multiple shared objects. Our approach integrates deep learning methods, graph-based algorithms, and optimization strategies to design a robust co-segmentation framework.

    2. Contrastive Fusion of Transformer and CNN-based Deep Features for Semantic Segmentation
      Advisor: Dr. Tushar Sandhan
      Semantic segmentation is essential in computer vision for accurately identifying and extracting objects or regions within an image. Despite advances in state-of-the-art models, many still require large external datasets for effective performance. We propose ContraFusionNet, a novel architecture that combines the spatial localization capabilities of CNNs with the global contextual understanding of Transformers. Using a student-teacher learning paradigm with contrastive loss, our model achieves robust segmentation even under limited data conditions. Notably, ContraFusionNet outperformed other methods when trained solely on the Cityscapes dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing data dependency. [Code]
