    Margi Pandya

    MS Student @ UCSD | Signal & Image Processing | Generative AI | Former Intern @ IIT Kanpur | Best Paper Awardee @ REEDCON

    Hi! I’m a Master’s student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UC San Diego, specializing in Machine Learning, Computer Vision, and Multimodal AI. I am passionate about building intelligent systems that go beyond perception — systems that understand, reason, and generate.

    I currently work on amodal segmentation and completion, exploring how diffusion models and vision-language alignment can help machines infer occluded or missing information in visual scenes. My broader research lies at the intersection of:

    • Amodal reasoning and generative modeling — using mixed-context diffusion sampling for inpainting and occlusion completion.
    • Multimodal learning and vision-language grounding — aligning generative objectives with semantic understanding using self-supervised techniques.
    • Efficient segmentation and co-saliency — leveraging Transformer-based architectures and class-activated maps across image sets.

    Previously, I worked on CAMFC: Class-Activated Multiple Foreground Co-segmentation, leveraging a customized Cityscapes dataset and high-performance computing resources. I’ve also built and fine-tuned LLM-driven AI tools, including a campaign generator integrating RAG pipelines and public APIs (Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn).

    During my undergraduate studies at NIT Trichy, I was awarded the Best Paper Award at REEDCON 2023 for my work in scene understanding and explainable learning. I also interned at IIT Kanpur, contributing to semantic segmentation and saliency detection research.

    I thrive in interdisciplinary teams and enjoy turning research into deployable AI solutions. I’m currently seeking opportunities in AI/ML engineering, computer vision, or applied research roles where I can contribute to building robust, real-world machine learning systems.
